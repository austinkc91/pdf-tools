How to Compress a PDF

  • Step 1: Drop your PDF file into the compressor (or click to browse)
  • Step 2: Choose compression level — Low (best quality), Medium (balanced), or High (smallest size)
  • Step 3: Click "Compress PDF" and download your smaller file

Why Compress PDFs?

  • Email attachments — Most email providers limit attachments to 25MB. Compression helps you send large documents.
  • Faster uploads — Smaller files upload faster to portals, forms, and cloud storage
  • Save storage — Reduce the space PDFs take on your device or cloud storage
  • Web publishing — Smaller PDFs load faster when embedded on websites

Three Compression Levels

  • Low: Minimal compression, best quality. Optimizes structure while preserving everything.
  • Medium: Balanced compression. Good size reduction while maintaining readability.
  • High: Maximum compression. Strips metadata and optimizes aggressively for smallest file size.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much smaller will my PDF be? ▼
Results depend on your PDF's content. PDFs with large images typically see 20-60% reduction. Already-optimized PDFs may see smaller improvements.
Will compression make my text blurry? ▼
No. Text and vector graphics remain perfectly crisp at all compression levels. Only embedded raster images may have slightly reduced quality at high compression.
Is there a file size limit? ▼
Since processing happens in your browser, the limit depends on your device's available memory. Most users can compress PDFs up to 100MB+.
Are my files safe? ▼
Absolutely. Your files are processed entirely on your device. Nothing is uploaded to any server. Your documents stay completely private.
